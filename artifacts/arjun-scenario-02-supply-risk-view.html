Supply Risk & Geopolitical Exposure Review

Indonesia has announced a new export levy on nickel matte, immediately putting pressure on two exposed business lines: Tata Motors EV battery cathodes and Tata Chemicals catalysts. The current exposure is about $140M per year, and supply is concentrated across two Indonesian smelters. The available responses are not equally attractive: the Philippines route is 15% costlier and takes four months to qualify, Australia is 22% costlier but available now, and the strategic stockpile can cover roughly 90 days. The core decision is whether to trigger stockpile use and move quickly toward Australia, or absorb the levy and negotiate while staying exposed to the current source base.
