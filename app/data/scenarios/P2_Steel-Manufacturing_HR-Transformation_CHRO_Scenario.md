**Domain:** Steel Manufacturing

## About this scenario

A skills and retention crisis at the intersection of a generous redundancy programme and an approaching commissioning deadline. The framework must handle a decision where the people dimension is primary, cost is secondary, and the 2027 operational deadline is non-negotiable.

---

## Scenario: EAF operators — external hire, accelerate reskilling, or raid another Tata entity?

The EAF requires approximately 400 specialist operators before commissioning in late 2027 — roles including EAF furnace operators, ladle metallurgy technicians, scrap quality supervisors, and electrical systems engineers. These are fundamentally different from the blast furnace skills held by the retained workforce.

By April 2026, Chris's reskilling programme has delivered 140 qualified personnel — 35% of the requirement. External hiring has yielded 60 candidates. The remaining ~200 roles have a 20-month window to fill. Two problems have emerged:

1. The UK talent pool for EAF operation is thin — the only comparable UK facility (7 Steel Cardiff) has poached three of Tata's most advanced trainees with salary offers 22% above Tata's EAF operator grade.
2. Tata Steel Netherlands (IJmuiden, also transitioning to EAF) has a surplus of experienced EAF operators in their commissioning cohort, available on a 12–18 month secondment. But the IJmuiden GM is reluctant; these are his best people, and his own ramp-up is 6 months behind target.

Three options:

**Option A — Accelerate the UK reskilling programme.** Double the training cohort intake, extend hours, bring in an external EAF training provider (e.g., Tenova's own operator training unit). Cost: ~£4.5M additional. Risk: quality of graduates may be lower under compressed timelines; still may not hit the number by 2027.

**Option B — Escalate the IJmuiden secondment request to group level.** Bypass the IJmuiden GM and escalate to Koushik Chatterjee (Group CFO) or T.V. Narendran to mandate the secondment. Gets experienced operators immediately. Risk: damages relationship with IJmuiden leadership; sets a precedent for group-mandated talent transfers that OpCo MDs will resist.

**Option C — Accept a hybrid — scale external hiring with a 15% salary premium.** Offer EAF operator roles at £52,000 (vs current grade of £45,000) to attract UK talent from non-steel industries with transferable electrical/arc skills. Cost: ~£1.4M/yr incremental on the base. Risk: creates a two-tier workforce within Tata Steel UK; blast furnace-era retained workers earning less than new EAF hires creates a fairness problem with the unions.

---

## The call

Accelerate internal reskilling, mandate group secondments, or accept a salary premium for external hires?

## Tension

Commissioning deadline vs workforce fairness vs inter-OpCo relationships within Tata Group.
