## Scenario: National Grid infrastructure — absorb the delay or escalate publicly?

Tata Steel UK's £1.25B EAF requires National Grid to deliver new high-voltage electrical infrastructure capable of powering a 400MW facility by end 2027. National Grid has a contractual obligation to deliver this by that date. In April 2026, Rajesh receives a private briefing from his Head of Energy (P8) that National Grid's internal programme has slipped by approximately 14 weeks — not yet confirmed publicly, not yet a formal contractual breach, but credible based on site survey data and conversations with National Grid project managers.

If the delay holds, it threatens to push EAF commissioning from Q4 2027 to Q1–Q2 2028 — a 3 to 6 month slip. Rajesh has three choices:

**Option A — Escalate immediately and publicly.** Formally notify UK Government (DBET), Welsh Government, and the Transition Board of the risk. Issue a holding statement to media. Apply contractual pressure on National Grid. Risk: this becomes a political story; government may seek to manage it; Tata is seen as blaming a UK national infrastructure body for the slip, creating tension in the grant relationship.

**Option B — Manage it quietly for 60 days.** Give National Grid the opportunity to recover the schedule internally. Maintain public commissioning target. Instruct the construction team to accelerate any parallel works that could absorb the delay. Risk: if the slip hardens and Tata knew for 60 days, the retrospective accountability is severe — especially given the grant conditions and political visibility.

**Option C — Partial escalation — notify UK Government privately; hold public position.** Briefing ministers directly but off the record, requesting they apply informal pressure on National Grid. Buys political cover if it later becomes public, without triggering media narrative. Risk: ministers may not act; Tata holds the risk during the 60-day window; government relationship depends on trust being maintained.

---

## The call

Escalate publicly, manage quietly for 60 days, or brief government privately while holding the public position?

## Tension

Project credibility and political relationships vs fiduciary duty to disclose a known risk that threatens grant conditions and the publicly committed 2027 date.
