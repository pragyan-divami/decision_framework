## Scenario: Transition gap cash shortfall — bridge internally, renegotiate customer contracts, or draw down grant early?

Tata Steel UK's downstream mills (hot strip mill, cold rolling, packaging lines) are currently running on imported slab from Tata Steel Netherlands and India. The slab import cost premium vs self-supplied blast furnace output is running at £38/tonne above the level assumed in the transition plan — driven by energy surcharges at IJmuiden and a stronger Indian rupee vs sterling since late 2025.

On current volumes of approximately 1.4 million tonnes through the downstream mills in the transition period, this is a £53M annualised overshoot vs plan. Over the 18 remaining months of the transition gap, the total overshoot is projected at £79M unless costs recover or volumes reduce.

Sian has three options:

**Option A — Request early drawdown of the £500M UK Government grant.** The grant is structured as milestone-linked, but the agreement has a provision for early access to up to £50M for "verified transition cost overruns." This would directly fund the gap. Risk: triggers a formal review of Tata's cost controls by DBET; signals financial stress to government at a politically sensitive time; creates precedent for further early draws.

**Option B — Renegotiate the slab import price with Tata Steel Netherlands.** Request a transfer-pricing concession of £20–25/tonne on the intra-group slab price, reducing the overshoot by ~55%. Risk: IJmuiden is itself under margin pressure; the Netherlands entity will resist; Koushik Chatterjee (Group CFO) will need to adjudicate — creating a group-level dispute at a bad time.

**Option C — Reduce transition gap volumes by 15%.** Manage the downstream mills at reduced capacity to cut total slab import spend. Accept the revenue reduction (~£28M). Risk: customer delivery commitments are affected; Stellantis and BMW may trigger contract review clauses; Priya Mehta's (P10) fragile transition-gap customer relationships are further stressed.

---

## The call

Draw down grant early, renegotiate the intra-group slab price, or cut transition volumes?

## Tension

Protecting the grant relationship and UK political confidence vs group-level commercial relationships vs customer commitments that underpin the post-2027 EAF revenue case.
