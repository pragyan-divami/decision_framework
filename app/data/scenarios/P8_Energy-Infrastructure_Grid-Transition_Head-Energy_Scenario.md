## About this scenario

The energy cost problem is the single biggest structural risk to the EAF business case. This scenario tests the framework on a decision where the financially optimal choice conflicts with the green credentials that justify customer premiums, and where a government negotiation adds a third dimension.

---

## Scenario: Renewable PPA vs grid electricity — lock in a long-term deal or wait for the government discount?

The EAF at full operation will consume approximately 400MW peak — making it the largest industrial electricity consumer in Wales. James has been negotiating two parallel tracks:

**Track 1 — Government industrial energy discount:** UK Government committed in the grant deal to reducing Tata Steel UK's electricity costs as part of the £500M package. The specific mechanism and quantum are still being finalised. James's intelligence suggests the discount could be worth £15–22/MWh — roughly 25–35% of the current UK industrial rate — but the paperwork is not expected to be signed until Q3 2026.

**Track 2 — Renewable PPA:** A consortium of two offshore wind developers (representing 480MW of capacity in the Celtic Sea, operational by mid-2027) has offered a 15-year PPA at £68/MWh — below the current forward curve of £78/MWh. This would also allow Tata to certify the EAF's electricity as renewable-sourced, which is critical for JLR's green steel credentials and the £8–12/tonne premium customers will pay for certified low-carbon steel.

Problem: If James signs the PPA at £68/MWh and the government discount later brings the grid electricity price below £65/MWh, he has locked in a more expensive deal than the unconstrained grid option. But if he waits for the government deal to close before signing the PPA, the wind consortium may withdraw the offer.

Three options:

**Option A — Sign the PPA now at £68/MWh.** Secures renewable certification and price certainty. If the government discount makes grid cheaper, Tata absorbs the differential — estimated maximum regret: £6–9M/yr over 15 years. Also: the PPA price is below the current forward curve regardless.

**Option B — Wait for the government discount to close before deciding.** Maximum financial optionality. Risk: PPA offer expires in 8 weeks; renewable certification is lost; JLR and automotive customers may require a different certification path that is slower and more expensive.

**Option C — Negotiate a PPA with a government-discount passthrough clause.** Ask the wind consortium to include a contractual mechanism that reduces the PPA price if and when a government discount exceeds a defined threshold. Risk: wind developers are unlikely to accept full passthrough; it is a complex clause that may kill the negotiation entirely.

---

## The call

Sign the PPA now, wait for the government deal, or negotiate a hybrid passthrough clause?

## Tension

Renewable credentials and price certainty now vs maximum financial flexibility pending a government negotiation that may produce a better deal.
