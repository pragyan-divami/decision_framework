## About this scenario

A board governance and political-relationship decision. Tests how the framework handles decisions where the correct answer depends almost entirely on the trust architecture between Tata, the UK Government, and the board's independent members — not on financial or operational data.

---

## Scenario: UK Government requests a board seat — accept, negotiate, or decline?

In April 2026, DBET writes formally to the Tata Steel UK board requesting the appointment of a UK Government observer to the board — a non-voting position with access to board papers and the right to attend all board meetings including any sessions discussing EAF construction progress, grant covenant compliance, and commercial strategy.

The request is framed as "in the interests of protecting the public investment of £500M." It is precedented: the UK Government holds observer positions on several other major grant-backed infrastructure projects.

Dr Henrik Adam must advise the board on how to respond. Three options:

**Option A — Accept the observer appointment unconditionally.** Full transparency; cements the cooperative relationship. Risk: Government observer has access to commercially sensitive customer negotiations (JLR, BMW, Stellantis), competitive strategy, and group-level capital discussions. Tata Steel Netherlands and India might object to a UK Government representative attending meetings where group strategy is discussed.

**Option B — Counter-propose a restructured reporting mechanism.** Offer quarterly deepened briefings for ministers, enhanced access to the Transition Board, and a dedicated government liaison officer — but without a board seat. Risk: Government may read this as Tata hiding something; the "why won't you give us a seat?" story is a bad media narrative.

**Option C — Accept a time-limited observer role for the construction phase only.** Government observer attends board meetings until EAF commissioning (end 2027), after which the arrangement is reviewed. This limits ongoing commercial exposure while satisfying the immediate accountability demand. Risk: requires a sunset clause that government may resist; creates an implicit suggestion that post-commissioning transparency decreases.

---

## The call

Accept unconditionally, counter-propose an enhanced reporting mechanism, or accept with a time-limited construction-phase-only scope?

## Tension

Protecting Tata's commercial confidentiality and group interests vs satisfying legitimate public accountability for £500M of taxpayer money.
