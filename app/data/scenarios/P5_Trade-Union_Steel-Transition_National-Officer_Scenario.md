## About this scenario

A union accountability crisis. Tests whether the framework can handle decisions where the stakeholder's primary KPIs are qualitative (member trust, institutional credibility) and the options involve tradeoffs between short-term confrontation and long-term cooperation.

---

## Scenario: EAF construction workforce — local hiring rate below commitment, Tata won't move

The July 2025 groundbreaking was accompanied by an informal — though not legally binding — commitment from Tata Steel UK that the EAF construction programme would prioritise local labour from Neath Port Talbot and the broader South Wales region. By April 2026, the actual local hiring rate on the Sir Robert McAlpine construction programme is 34% — well below the 60%+ that Community Union and Cllr Hunt's council anticipated.

The shortfall is partly structural: specialist civil engineering and demolition trades needed for the brownfield blast furnace clearance are simply not available locally in sufficient numbers. But it is also partly commercial: Sir Robert McAlpine and its regional supply chain subcontractors have brought in cheaper labour from elsewhere.

Alun has three options:

**Option A — Escalate formally to the Transition Board.** Table the local hiring data at the next Transition Board meeting, naming the shortfall publicly and requesting Tata commit to a binding 60% local labour covenant with enforcement mechanisms. Risk: antagonises Tata and Sir Robert McAlpine at a time when Community Union needs both parties cooperative on EAF operator terms negotiations; risks being seen as obstructionist on a project the union publicly supported.

**Option B — Negotiate a quiet improvement agreement with Tata HR.** Work through Chris Jaques (P2) to agree a revised local hiring target (45–50%) and a monitoring mechanism, without publicising the current underperformance. Risk: members who lost their jobs are watching. If the data becomes public and Alun knew and said nothing, his credibility with the Port Talbot membership is gone.

**Option C — Make the data public through media, then negotiate.** Brief ITV Wales and the Senedd Research team on the local hiring figures, creating political pressure on Tata and Sir Robert McAlpine before formal negotiations. Risk: the confrontational approach damages the "bittersweet but collaborative" narrative Alun has maintained; ministers may be annoyed he didn't come to them first; Tata's cooperation on EAF operator wages and conditions becomes harder.

---

## The call

Escalate to the Transition Board, negotiate quietly with Tata HR, or go public first?

## Tension

Institutional accountability and member trust vs the cooperative relationships that Alun needs for the more consequential EAF operator employment terms negotiation in 2027.
