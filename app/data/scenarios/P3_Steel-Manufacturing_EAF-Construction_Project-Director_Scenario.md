## About this scenario

A classic programme management crisis: a scope change request from the primary contractor that trades short-term cost for long-term performance, at a point in the programme where saying yes or no both have serious consequences.

---

## Scenario: Tenova scope change — upgraded transformer or maintain the contract spec?

Nine months into construction, Tenova's engineering team presents a formal change request (CR-047). The EAF's electrical transformer specified in the original contract (at £8.3M) has a maximum power input of 140 MVA. Tenova's detailed design has identified that a 160 MVA transformer would allow a tap-to-tap cycle time improvement from 42 minutes to approximately 38 minutes — a 9.5% throughput improvement that, at 3.2M t/yr capacity, is worth approximately £14M/yr in additional revenue at full utilisation.

The upgrade costs £3.1M more and requires a 9-week programme extension at the transformer installation phase — manageable within the current schedule float, but only if no other delay occurs. If anything else slips in the next 20 months, this 9 weeks becomes critical path.

Three options:

**Option A — Accept the change, absorb into programme float.** The long-term return (£14M/yr) vs the one-off cost (£3.1M) is a compelling NPV case. Risk: the 9-week extension consumes all remaining schedule float; one more slip and the commissioning date moves.

**Option B — Reject the change, hold the original spec.** Protects the schedule and the grant milestone. The 42-minute cycle still hits the 3.2M t/yr target. Risk: leaving provable value on the table; Tenova relationship strained by rejecting a well-reasoned engineering improvement.

**Option C — Accept the change but require Tenova to absorb the schedule impact.** Negotiate the upgrade at Tata's cost (£3.1M) but require Tenova to maintain the original commissioning milestone by accelerating elsewhere in the programme. Risk: Tenova may accept and then claim force majeure on the acceleration — creating a dispute.

---

## The call

Accept the upgrade and absorb the schedule risk, reject it and protect the date, or accept and negotiate Tenova-borne acceleration?

## Tension

Long-term operational value vs schedule certainty on a programme where the 2027 commissioning date is a grant condition and a political commitment.
