## About this scenario

A supply security decision under genuine market pressure, with an irreversibility dimension. The EAF cannot operate without scrap — this is the feedstock equivalent of the blast furnace's iron ore problem, but harder to solve because the market structure doesn't yet exist.

---

## Scenario: Long-term scrap contract — lock in now at a premium, or wait for the market to develop?

The EAF will require 2.0–2.5 million tonnes of high-quality, low-residual scrap per year from 2028. As of April 2026, Kate has contracted 820,000 tonnes per year from 11 UK scrap merchants — 41% of the requirement. The remaining 1.2–1.7 million tonnes is uncontracted.

The UK scrap market is in flux. Two dynamics are colliding:

1. **Price spike risk:** European EAF operators (Salzgitter, Thyssenkrupp, ArcelorMittal) are all competing for the same high-quality flat-steel scrap. Spot prices for prime shredded (PSX) grade are up 18% year-on-year. Locking in long-term contracts now, at today's prices, looks expensive. But if the market tightens further, the EAF's operating cost model becomes unviable.

2. **Infrastructure gap:** The December 2025 UK Steel Circular Steel Sub-Committee report warned that high-quality scrap supply at the required volume cannot be secured without investment in UK shredding and sorting infrastructure. One UK scrap group (C&B Metals, 180 employees, Hull) has approached Kate offering a 10-year offtake commitment in exchange for a £12M co-investment in a new high-specification shredder. This would guarantee 350,000 tonnes/yr of PSX-grade scrap but ties Tata to a long-term infrastructure stake.

Three options:

**Option A — Lock in long-term contracts now, accepting current premium pricing.** Contract the remaining 1.2M tonnes at spot +8–12% (£38–45/tonne above index). Provides supply certainty. Cost: ~£45–54M/yr above a hypothetical perfect-market price; adds ~£14/tonne to EAF operating cost vs plan.

**Option B — Take the C&B Metals co-investment deal.** Commit £12M capital, secure 350,000 tonnes/yr PSX-grade for 10 years at index price. Then contract remaining 850,000 tonnes at market rates. Total contracted: 1.17M tonnes + 820K existing = ~2M tonnes. Risk: £12M capital commitment needs CFO approval; Tata becomes a partial infrastructure investor in a sector it doesn't understand; if C&B Metals fails, the investment is stranded.

**Option C — Wait 12 months before contracting the balance.** Bet that the scrap market will normalise as more UK infrastructure comes online. Continue building relationships with merchants without committing. Risk: December 2027 commissioning arrives with uncontracted scrap; spot purchases in a tight market could push EAF operating costs 20–25% above plan in year 1.

---

## The call

Lock in now at a premium, co-invest in infrastructure for price certainty, or wait 12 months?

## Tension

Supply security vs operating cost discipline vs capital exposure in an infrastructure sector outside Tata's core competence.
