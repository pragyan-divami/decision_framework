## About this scenario

The ultimate test of the EAF's commercial rationale. A scenario where the product capability gap between what the EAF can make and what the most valuable customers need is real, narrowing, but not yet closed — and a customer is forcing the issue before Tata is ready to answer.

---

## Scenario: BMW demands grade commitment before 2027 — can the EAF deliver AHSS or not?

BMW UK (MINI, Rolls-Royce) is Tata Steel UK's second-largest automotive customer after JLR. In April 2026, BMW's procurement director delivers a formal request: Tata must confirm by Q3 2026 whether the Port Talbot EAF will be able to supply Advanced High-Strength Steel (AHSS) grades for MINI body panels — specifically DP1000 and DP1200 grades — when the EAF is commissioned.

If Tata cannot confirm this by Q3, BMW will begin a qualification process with an alternative European supplier. This would represent approximately £48M/yr in annual revenue from 2028 — 8% of the EAF's revenue target.

The problem: the EAF's ability to produce DP1000/1200 grades depends on scrap quality (copper residuals must be below 0.08% for these grades — tighter than the standard PSX spec Kate Ellison is contracting). Tata's metallurgical team believes the ladle metallurgy furnaces (LMFs) under construction will achieve this spec, but the commissioning tests won't be complete until Q2 2028. There is no confirmed data.

Three options:

**Option A — Confirm the commitment to BMW unconditionally.** Tell BMW DP1000/DP1200 will be available from EAF commissioning. Keep the customer; accelerate the metallurgical validation programme internally. Risk: if the LMF commissioning tests fail to achieve the required residuals, Tata has committed to a product it cannot deliver; contract penalties and customer relationship damage follow.

**Option B — Decline to confirm and risk losing BMW.** Give BMW an honest answer: Tata cannot guarantee AHSS grades until Q2 2028 metallurgical validation is complete. Offer transitional supply from IJmuiden in the interim. Risk: BMW begins competitive qualification; Priya loses the account; £48M/yr revenue disappears from the EAF business case; sends a signal to other automotive customers about EAF grade capability.

**Option C — Commit to a phased qualification programme with BMW jointly.** Propose a co-development programme: BMW's metallurgical team and Tata's work together on the LMF commissioning spec from Q3 2026, with BMW given first sight of validation data in Q2 2028 and a right of first refusal on AHSS supply. BMW shares the grade development risk; Tata keeps the relationship without an unconditional commitment. Risk: BMW may decline the co-development offer and demand a straight yes/no anyway.

---

## The call

Commit unconditionally, decline and manage the loss, or propose a joint qualification programme?

## Tension

Revenue security and customer retention vs technical honesty about a product capability gap that exists — and may be resolved, but only after commissioning.
