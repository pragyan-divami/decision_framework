# Tata Steel UK EAF Transformation
## Contracts & Commercial Variance Scenario Pack

This pack contains a set of scenario briefs designed for the Tata Steel UK Port Talbot EAF transformation context.
Each scenario is structured for decision-matrix use and includes:
- trigger
- options A/B/C
- KPI families
- decision tension

---

## Scenario 01 · Tenova Exceptional Cost Variation Dispute

### Trigger
Tenova submits a major composite variation claim tied to steel cost escalation, specialist labour scarcity, and a compliance-driven redesign to the fume treatment package. Tata accepts that part of the claim may be valid in principle, but disputes the market-risk elements. Tenova signals a possible suspension right if the disputed amount is not handled within the contractual payment window.

### Decision tension
**Programme continuity vs contractual discipline**

### Options
**A. Pay under protest**  
Release the disputed amount to protect delivery momentum, while reserving rights for later recovery.

**B. Pay only the uncontested amount**  
Approve the clearly justifiable element and formally dispute the balance.

**C. Force negotiated settlement / formal dispute path**  
Push the issue into structured commercial resolution, accepting near-term delay risk for better long-term precedent control.

### KPI families
- Schedule float remaining
- Capital contingency consumed
- Grant covenant exposure
- Contractor cooperation / relationship health
- Customer confidence / offtake confidence

## Scenario 02 · ABB Electrical Interface Change Claim

### Trigger
ABB argues that late design coordination changes at the interface between the EAF package and site electrical systems have shifted engineering, routing, and commissioning activities outside original scope. ABB submits a change order for rework, accelerated testing, and site modifications.

### Decision tension
**Speed of energisation vs scope-boundary discipline**

### Options
**A. Approve the change quickly**  
Prioritise energisation certainty and absorb the cost to protect commissioning readiness.

**B. Reject and force package-boundary clarification**  
Challenge the claim on the basis that the issue is an interface ambiguity rather than a compensable variation.

**C. Split liability across parties**  
Negotiate a shared-cost solution involving ABB, the main contractor, and Tata project controls.

### KPI families
- Electrical commissioning readiness
- Scope change order value
- Interface issue closure rate
- Energisation critical-path status
- Capex variance

## Scenario 03 · National Grid Delay and Cost-Recovery Dispute

### Trigger
National Grid advises that a critical enabling infrastructure element may be delivered late due to upstream equipment lead times. Tata’s core works may still progress, but full energisation and commissioning would slip. Tata’s team considers whether to preserve delay-damage or cost-recovery rights.

### Decision tension
**Collaborative dependency management vs formal claims preservation**

### Options
**A. Escalate contractually now**  
Issue formal notices, preserve claims, and prepare for recovery of prolongation and transition costs.

**B. Stay collaborative and avoid formal escalation**  
Work jointly with National Grid to recover the programme without damaging the relationship.

**C. Run a dual-track approach**  
Preserve formal rights quietly while keeping the working relationship outwardly cooperative.

### KPI families
- National Grid synchronisation status
- Commissioning date at risk
- Grant milestone exposure
- Transition operating cost premium
- External dependency risk index

## Scenario 04 · Scrap Supply Contract Reopener

### Trigger
A major scrap supplier invokes a reopener clause after market prices rise sharply and higher-quality low-residual scrap becomes harder to secure. The supplier can still provide volume, but not the chemistry mix needed for higher-value automotive-grade output unless Tata accepts a premium and revised quality terms.

### Decision tension
**Volume security vs quality security**

### Options
**A. Accept the reopener and secure quality**  
Pay more to lock in the chemistry profile needed for premium downstream products.

**B. Resist the reset and hold current terms**  
Protect commercial discipline and challenge the supplier’s attempt to reopen pricing.

**C. Restructure the portfolio**  
Maintain some incumbent supply while using bridge contracts or alternate suppliers to cover the quality gap.

### KPI families
- Contracted scrap volume secured
- Scrap quality compliance rate
- Scrap cost vs plan
- Supplier concentration risk
- Customer-grade fulfilment risk

## Scenario 05 · Automotive Offtake Longstop Renegotiation

### Trigger
A key automotive customer requests revised protections around start-date certainty, quality certification milestones, price protection, and exit rights after hearing of possible programme slippage. Tata must decide how far to go to retain the customer without weakening future customer negotiations.

### Decision tension
**Anchor-customer retention vs downside contractual exposure**

### Options
**A. Offer stronger protections and secure the volume**  
Protect strategic customer relationships even if the contract becomes more one-sided.

**B. Hold the line on terms**  
Preserve commercial discipline and avoid creating a precedent across all customers.

**C. Selectively tailor concessions**  
Give limited protections to the most strategic customer accounts while avoiding a portfolio-wide reset.

### KPI families
- Offtake volume contracted
- Customer revenue at risk
- Customer trust / account sentiment
- Qualification activity at competitors
- Green-steel certification readiness

## Scenario 06 · DBET Grant Covenant Disclosure Threshold

### Trigger
A cluster of approved changes plus one disputed contractor claim pushes total overrun risk close to the point where DBET could view the programme as drifting outside its original grant assumptions. Finance must decide whether to notify proactively or wait until the commercial position is more crystallised.

### Decision tension
**Grant trust and compliance vs short-term funding flexibility**

### Options
**A. Proactively notify DBET**  
Protect trust, preserve defensibility, and reduce the risk of later accusations of concealment.

**B. Wait for greater certainty**  
Avoid triggering a formal review before schedule and commercial implications are fully proven.

**C. Prepare disclosure while holding internally**  
Build the evidence pack, legal view, and notice structure now, but delay sending until a defined trigger is crossed.

### KPI families
- Grant drawdown compliance
- Grant clawback risk
- Capital programme contingency remaining
- Debt / working-capital headroom
- Governance risk index

## Recommended use in the decision framework

These six scenarios cover the main contracts/commercial variance types in the Tata Steel UK transformation programme:
- contractor claim and suspension risk
- interface change-order dispute
- external dependency delay claim
- raw-material contract reopener
- customer offtake renegotiation
- grant / covenant disclosure tension

Together they give good coverage for CEOs, CFOs, project leaders, procurement, energy, commercial, governance, union, and public-sector personas.
