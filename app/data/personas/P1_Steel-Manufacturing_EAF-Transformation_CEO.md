# P1 · Rajesh Nair

**Domain:** Steel Manufacturing
**Platform:** EAF Transformation Programme
**Persona:** CEO, Tata Steel UK

---

## Role

Chief Executive Officer, Tata Steel UK. Based at Port Talbot. Reports to T.V. Narendran (CEO, Tata Steel Group, Mumbai) and to the Tata Steel UK Board chaired by Dr Henrik Adam. Member of the Tata Steel UK/Port Talbot Transition Board.

---

## Background

Rajesh Nair is the CEO of Tata Steel UK, the entity responsible for delivering the £1.25 billion EAF transformation at Port Talbot — the largest investment in the UK steel industry in decades. He was present at the July 14 2025 groundbreaking ceremony alongside Tata Group Chairman Natarajan Chandrasekaran, Business Secretary Jonathan Reynolds, and Wales Secretary Jo Stevens.

He carries ultimate UK accountability for: delivering the EAF by end-2027 commissioning target, maintaining Tata's relationship with the UK and Welsh Governments under the £500M grant conditions, managing the human and community cost of 2,500+ redundancies, and returning Tata Steel UK to financial sustainability after £4 billion in losses since 2007.

---

## Context: the project

- **Investment:** £1.25B total (£750M Tata, £500M UK Government grant)
- **Contractor:** Tenova (EAF), ABB (electrical), Clecim (downstream)
- **Capacity:** 3.2 million tonnes/yr — one of Europe's largest EAFs
- **Feedstock:** UK-sourced scrap steel (replacing imported iron ore and coking coal)
- **CO₂ impact:** –90% from Port Talbot site (–5M tonnes/yr)
- **Construction start:** July 2025
- **Commissioning target:** End of 2027
- **Workforce:** ~5,000 retained; ~2,500 left through voluntary redundancy

## Decision style notes

Rajesh operates at the intersection of Indian corporate culture (group accountability, long-term horizon) and Welsh/UK political culture (visible community commitment, short-cycle media). His decisions tend to be diplomatic in framing but strategically firm. He will not move the 2027 date publicly even under construction pressure. He is accountable enough to the UK political class that community and workforce optics constrain purely commercial decisions.
