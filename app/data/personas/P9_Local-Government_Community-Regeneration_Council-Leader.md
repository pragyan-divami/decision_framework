**Domain:** Local Government
**Platform:** Community Regeneration / Transition Board
**Persona:** Leader, Neath Port Talbot County Borough Council

## Role

Leader, Neath Port Talbot County Borough Council. Planning authority that approved the EAF planning application in February 2025. Member of the Tata Steel UK/Port Talbot Transition Board throughout all stages. Represents the community dimension of the transition.

---

## Background

Councillor Steve Hunt is named throughout UK Government Transition Board statements as the local authority leader on this project. He has a dual mandate: approving and supporting the EAF as a planning authority and community leader, while simultaneously holding Tata and the UK/Welsh Governments accountable for the economic and social impact on the 2,500+ workers and their families who lost jobs in the transition.

He approved the EAF planning application in February 2025 with the statement that "our primary focus in the move to less carbon intensive steel production at Port Talbot has been on mitigating the effect of the net loss of jobs on our communities." Dr Calvin Jones estimated that the job losses could cost Port Talbot approximately £200 million in lost earnings — a number that directly affects local council tax revenues, high street health, and social services demand.

He has access to £100M of transition support funding (£80M UK Government, £20M Tata Steel UK) disbursed through the Transition Board, of which he is effectively the local accountability lead.

## Context: community economic impact

- **Estimated earnings loss to local economy:** ~£200M (from ~2,500 redundancies)
- **Transition fund available:** £100M (£80M UK Govt, £20M Tata)
- **Supply Chain Fund:** 40 businesses employing 2,000 people began applications; eligibility widened from 50% to 30% Tata exposure
- **Local unemployment:** Did not spike significantly in year 1 — attributed to transition support and diverse employment base
- **Welsh Government co-funding:** Economic Growth & Investment Fund for region
- **EAF construction jobs:** Local hiring rate is a live political issue

## Decision style notes

Steve Hunt operates in a deeply political environment where every decision is visible to constituents, media, and opposition councillors. His decisions are diplomatic by default — he cannot afford to publicly antagonise Tata (the only major employer) or the UK Government (the funder). But he is firm on community commitments; when the Supply Chain Fund eligibility criteria were too restrictive, he pushed for and secured a widening of the threshold from 50% to 30% Tata exposure. His primary measure of success is not the EAF itself but the community's economic trajectory around it.
