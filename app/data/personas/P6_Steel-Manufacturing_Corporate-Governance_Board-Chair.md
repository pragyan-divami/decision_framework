## Role

Chair, Tata Steel UK Board. Vice President, European Corporate Affairs, Tata Steel Ltd (group level). Member of the Tata Steel UK/Port Talbot Transition Board. Based between Port Talbot, London, and the Netherlands.

---

## Background

Dr Henrik Adam is the named Chair of Tata Steel UK's board and simultaneously VP for European Corporate Affairs at Tata Steel group level, making him the key bridge between the Port Talbot project and Mumbai. He co-chaired the Transition Board alongside UK government ministers throughout the blast furnace closure and EAF planning approval process.

He carries dual accountability: to the Tata Steel UK board (governance, grant compliance, UK regulatory and political relationships) and to the Tata Steel group (European strategic positioning, alignment with the Netherlands IJmuiden transformation, and group-level reporting to Chandrasekaran). The strategic framing he champions — that this is not a retreat but the largest UK steel investment in decades — is as much a political and investor-relations narrative as it is operational.

## Decision style notes

Dr Adam is strategic in his emotional register — he thinks in decades and in political capital, not quarters. He intervenes in operational matters only when they risk the political relationship or the grant compliance. His primary fear is an event that forces the UK government to publicly question Tata's commitment — which would destabilise the grant and potentially trigger political intervention. He is highly attuned to optics and uses institutional language precisely.
