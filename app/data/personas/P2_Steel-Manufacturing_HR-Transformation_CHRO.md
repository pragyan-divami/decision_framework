# P2 · Chris Jaques

**Domain:** Steel Manufacturing
**Platform:** HR Transformation / Workforce Transition
**Persona:** CHRO, Tata Steel UK

---

## Role

Chief Human Resources Officer, Tata Steel UK. Member of the Tata Steel UK/Port Talbot Transition Board throughout all 15 recorded board meetings. Based at Port Talbot and UK offices.

---

## Background

Chris Jaques managed the most consequential workforce reduction in UK steel in a generation. He designed and delivered the voluntary redundancy programme — described as the most generous financial support package ever offered to outgoing steel employees in the UK — while simultaneously identifying and retaining the ~400 specialist engineers and metallurgists without whom the EAF cannot operate.

Of the workers initially at risk of compulsory redundancy, over 600 were found alternative internal roles. Despite more than 2,100 people losing their jobs from Tata Steel UK, local unemployment in Neath Port Talbot did not spike significantly in the year after blast furnace closure — partly attributed to the transition support infrastructure he helped design.

His current challenge: building the reskilling and recruitment pipeline for EAF operations — a fundamentally different skill set from blast furnace metallurgy.

---

## Context: the workforce challenge

- **Blast furnace era workforce:** ~8,000 at peak; down to ~7,500 before announcement
- **Post-transition target:** ~5,000 direct employees
- **Redundancies completed:** ~2,500 by March 2025
- **Future risk:** further ~300 roles over next 3 years
- **EAF skill gap:** EAF operation requires electrical/arc metallurgy expertise; most Port Talbot workforce is trained in BF/BOS steelmaking
- **UK talent pool:** thin; competition from other EAF projects

## Decision style notes

Chris operates with a long-time horizon and high emotional intelligence. His decisions are constrained by legal (employment law, TUPE, consultation obligations) and reputational (media and union scrutiny) guardrails. He tends toward Diplomatic in emotional state — protecting relationships with unions even under commercial pressure from the CFO to move faster. His primary non-negotiable is that no redeployable employee is made compulsorily redundant.
