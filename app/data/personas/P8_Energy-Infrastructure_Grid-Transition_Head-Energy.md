# P8 · James Whitfield

**Domain:** Energy Infrastructure
**Platform:** Grid Transition / Industrial Energy
**Persona:** Head of Energy & Grid Infrastructure, Tata Steel UK

---

## Role

Head of Energy and Grid Infrastructure, Tata Steel UK. Reports to the CFO and to the EAF Project Director. Works daily with National Grid, UK Government energy policy teams, and energy trading desks.

---

## Background

James Whitfield is a composite persona representing the energy function that owns what may be the single biggest cost and risk variable in the EAF business case. The Port Talbot EAF will be the largest industrial electricity consumer in Wales. National Grid has a contractual commitment to build new electrical infrastructure capable of powering the 3.2 million tonne EAF by end 2027 — and James is the interface on Tata's side for that programme.

UK industrial electricity prices are approximately 50% higher than in continental Europe — which is the primary reason European steelmakers have historically preferred operating EAFs in Germany, the Netherlands, or Spain over the UK. The UK Government committed to reducing industrial energy costs as part of the £500M grant deal. James is negotiating the detailed energy discount mechanism and a long-term Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) for renewable electricity that would both reduce cost and support the EAF's green steel credentials.

---

## Context: energy specifics

- **EAF power demand:** ~400MW peak draw (equivalent to a small city)
- **National Grid obligation:** New infrastructure to Port Talbot by end 2027 (parallel programme to EAF construction)
- **UK vs EU energy premium:** ~50% higher industrial electricity price than continental benchmark
- **Government commitment:** Industrial energy discount as part of grant deal — details under negotiation
- **Renewable PPA:** JLR and automotive customers expect green steel; requires renewable electricity sourcing
- **Energy cost estimate:** ~£80M/yr at current UK industrial rates — the largest single operating cost of the EAF
